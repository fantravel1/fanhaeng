What Is FanHaengScore™?

FanHaengScore™ is our proprietary 1–100 scoring system, built from the ground up for one specific audience: fans traveling to Korea. It is applied to neighborhoods, experiences, events, filming locations, fan cafés, concert venues, and every other type of destination covered across FanHaeng.

The system exists to answer a single question: “Is this worth it for a fan?”

This is not a generic tourism rating. You will not find the same criteria used by hotel review sites or restaurant apps. FanHaengScore was built specifically for fandom travel — evaluating locations and experiences through the lens of cultural significance, fan infrastructure, emotional resonance, and practical accessibility for international visitors.

Every score you see on FanHaeng is the result of editorial evaluation across seven weighted dimensions. No algorithms guessing at relevance. No user-submitted star ratings with no context. Just a transparent, repeatable methodology designed by fans, for fans.

Every FanHaengScore is built on transparent, editorial evaluation — not algorithms or paid placement.

The Seven Scoring Dimensions

Every FanHaengScore is a weighted composite of seven distinct dimensions. Each dimension is scored individually, then combined based on its assigned weight to produce a final score out of 100. Here is exactly what we measure and why it matters.

🏯

1. Cultural Impact — Weight: 20%

How significant is this experience in K-pop, K-drama, or broader Korean pop culture? Does it connect to real fan moments, historic events, or cultural milestones that fans around the world recognize?

Weight in final score20%
👥

2. Fan Density — Weight: 15%

Will you find other fans here? Is there fan infrastructure — dedicated cafés, merch shops, cupsleeve events, or regular fan gatherings? A high fan density score means you will feel the community energy around you.

Weight in final score15%
🚇

3. Accessibility — Weight: 15%

How easy is it to get there by public transit? Are there language barriers? English signage? Clear directions from a major subway station? We evaluate every location from the perspective of an international visitor navigating Seoul's transit system.

Weight in final score15%
🛡

4. Safety — Weight: 15%

How safe is the area for all travelers, including solo visitors and women traveling alone? Is it well-lit, well-connected to transit, staffed, and free of known risks? Safety is never compromised for a higher overall score.

Weight in final score15%
💖

5. “You’ll Remember This” Factor — Weight: 15%

Emotional resonance. Will this moment stay with you long after you leave Korea? The goosebumps dimension — the thing that separates “I went there” from “I will never forget that.”

Weight in final score15%
💰

6. Cost Reality — Weight: 10%

Is this affordable for most fan travelers? Are there hidden costs — mandatory purchases, premium upsells, or transport expenses that aren’t obvious upfront? We evaluate value honestly, factoring in what fans actually spend.

Weight in final score10%
🌱

7. Ease for First-Timers — Weight: 10%

Can a first-time Korea visitor navigate this experience without prior knowledge or local help? Are expectations clear before arrival? This dimension ensures we never assume familiarity that new visitors don’t have.

Weight in final score10%
Bonus Dimension: Photo Value

Photo Value is tracked as an optional bonus tag on applicable experiences. It evaluates how photogenic or “Instagram-worthy” a location is — but it is tagged separately and not included in the main composite score. A location can have a moderate FanHaengScore but excellent Photo Value, and vice versa.

How to Read the Scores

FanHaengScores range from 1 to 100. Here is what each tier means in practice, and how we recommend you use them when planning your trip.

90 – 100: Must-Experience

Plan your trip around this.

90+

These are the defining fan experiences in Korea. High cultural significance, excellent infrastructure, deeply memorable. If you only have a few days, start here.

80 – 89: Highly Recommended

Worth making time for.

80+

Excellent experiences that strongly deliver on fan expectations. Not quite trip-defining, but you will be glad you made the effort. Great for a full week itinerary.

70 – 79: Good Experience

Fit it in if you can.

70+

Solid fan experiences with some caveats — maybe access is tricky, or the experience is better for specific fandoms. Worth doing if your schedule allows.

60 – 69: Decent

Nice but not essential.

60+

Adequate experiences that may appeal to specific interests but do not deliver the full package. Consider these if you have extra time and a specific reason to go.

Below 60: Consider Skipping

Unless it’s personal to you.

<60

Low FanHaengScores usually indicate limited fan infrastructure, difficult access, or experiences that do not live up to expectations. That said, personal meaning always trumps a number — if your bias has a connection to this place, go.

What FanHaengScore Is NOT

  • Not a “best places in Korea” list. FanHaengScore is fan-specific. A location can be one of the most beautiful places in Korea and still receive a moderate score if it lacks fan infrastructure or cultural relevance to K-pop and K-drama.
  • Not paid placement. No business, venue, tourism board, or entertainment company can pay to influence a FanHaengScore. Every score is editorial and independent.
  • Not permanent. Scores are living ratings. They update when new information emerges — new transit access, venue closures, shifts in fan activity, safety changes, or community feedback.
  • Not the only factor. Your personal fandom matters more than any number. FanHaengScore provides a framework, but your connection to a group, a drama, or a memory can make any location a 100 for you.
Remember This

A location with a FanHaengScore of 70 for BTS fans might be a 95 for you if your bias trained there. The score is a starting point — your heart is the final filter.

The Philosophy Behind the Score

FanHaengScore was not created to rank experiences against each other or to tell you what to like. It was created to give you clarity. When you are planning a trip with limited time and budget — often from thousands of miles away — you deserve a system that respects your intelligence and tells you exactly what to expect.

🤝

Built by Fans, for Fans

Every dimension and weight was developed by people who have stood in concert queues, navigated fan cafés in a foreign language, and felt the specific anxiety of planning a fan trip. This is not theoretical — it is lived.

🏯

Cultural Respect Is Always a Factor

We never score a location highly if visiting it disrespects Korean culture, invades privacy, or encourages behavior that harms the relationship between fans and the communities they visit.

🛡

Safety Is Never Compromised

Safety carries a 15% weight and acts as a floor. An otherwise excellent experience will be scored down if it presents safety concerns for solo travelers, women, or first-time international visitors.

💬

Community Feedback Matters

We update scores based on real fan reports and experiences shared with us. If something has changed since our last evaluation, the community helps us catch it.

🔍

Transparency Is Non-Negotiable

You can see every dimension, every weight, and every reasoning behind every score. We do not hide methodology behind vague “editorial discretion.” If we scored it, we can explain it.

🔄

Scores Are Living Documents

Korea’s fan landscape changes constantly — new venues open, events shift neighborhoods, transit lines extend. FanHaengScores update to reflect reality, not a snapshot from years ago.
